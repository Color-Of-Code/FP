# Free Monoid

The **free monoid** on a set $S$ is the "most general" monoid generated by $S$: the set of finite
sequences (lists) of elements of $S$, with concatenation as the operation and the empty list as the
unit. In programming the free monoid is simply `[a]` — lists are the canonical example of a monoid
precisely because they are free.

## Definition

### Universal property

A **free monoid** on a set $S$ is a monoid $(M^*, \cdot, \varepsilon)$ together with a function
$\eta : S \to M^*$ (the **inclusion** / generator map) such that: for every monoid $(M, \cdot, e)$
and every function $f : S \to M$, there exists a **unique monoid homomorphism**
$\bar{f} : M^* \to M$ making the diagram commute:

$$f = \bar{f} \circ \eta$$

This is the universal property of the free monoid: it is initial among all monoids receiving a
function from $S$.

### Concrete construction

$$S^* = \{\varepsilon\} \cup S \cup S^2 \cup S^3 \cup \cdots$$

The elements are finite lists (words) over $S$; the monoid operation is concatenation; the unit is
the empty list $\varepsilon$.

### Adjunction

The free monoid construction is the left adjoint to the **forgetful functor**
$U :
\mathbf{Mon} \to \mathbf{Set}$ that strips the monoid structure:

$$F : \mathbf{Set} \rightleftharpoons \mathbf{Mon} : U \qquad F \dashv U$$

$$F(S) = S^* \qquad \mathbf{Mon}(F(S), M) \cong \mathbf{Set}(S, U(M))$$

This is the categorical meaning of "free": $F$ is left adjoint to forgetting.

## Laws

As with any adjunction:

- **Unit** $\eta_S : S \to U(F(S)) = S^*$ is the inclusion $s \mapsto [s]$ (singleton list)
- **Counit** $\varepsilon_M : F(U(M)) = M^{**} \to M$ is the unique homomorphism that "evaluates" a
  list of monoid elements by multiplying them together (the universal fold)
- The triangle identities hold, confirming $F \dashv U$

The free monoid $S^*$ satisfies **no equations beyond the monoid laws** — it is the monoid with only
the axioms and nothing extra. This is the meaning of "free".

## FP Analog

| CT concept                                       | Haskell / FP                                                                          |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Free monoid $S^*$                                | `[a]` — list is the free monoid over `a`                                              |
| Inclusion $\eta_S$                               | `pure :: a -> [a]` (singleton list)                                                   |
| Unique homomorphism $\bar{f}$                    | `foldMap f :: [a] -> m` for any `Monoid m`                                            |
| Forgetful functor $U$                            | Treating a monoid as a plain set (discarding the ops)                                 |
| $\mathbf{Mon}(F(S), M) \cong \mathbf{Set}(S, M)$ | `foldMap` is the only homomorphism from `[a]` to `m` given `a -> m`                   |
| `mconcat`                                        | $\varepsilon_M$ applied to `[[a]] -> [a]`, or `fold :: Monoid m => [m] -> m`          |
| `DList`, `Seq`                                   | Alternative free-monoid representations (same universal property, better performance) |

The universal property of the free monoid is why `foldMap` is the "right" fold: it is the unique
monoid homomorphism, and the whole interface of `Foldable` flows from this.
